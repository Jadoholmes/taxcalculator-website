Why is deferred compensation put into place
This is put into place as a perk the employer offers. This is a perk because it reduces taxes for the employees since it reduces the amount of taxable income. Although it may seem the same since the money is getting taken out of the check but that money taken out from def comp will eventually come back to you the alternative would be not getting that money at all.
When do you get the money back
Typically you get the money back from deferred compensation when you are no longer an employee of the employer who offered the def comp. That would mean retirement or either when you get a job change.

What are the types of def comp plans

  • Qualified Deferred Compensation Plans
    ‚Äç     Qualified deferred compensation plans are pension plans governed by theEmployee Retirement Income Security Act (ERISA), a key set of federal regulations for retirement plans.

    They include 401(k) plans and 403(b) plans.

    A company that has such a plan in place must offer it to all employees, though not to independent contractors.

    Funds in qualifying deferred compensation plans are for the sole benefit of their recipients. Creditors cannot access the funds if the company goes bankrupt. Contributions to the plans are capped by law.
  • Non-Qualifying deffered compinsation plans

    Non-qualified deferred compensation (NQDC) plans are also known as 409(a) plans and "golden handcuffs,"As the name implies, they are typically offered only to top-level executives and key talent that the company really wants to retain.

    They do not have to be offered to all employees. They also have no caps on contributions.

How do def comp and social security work

Def comp plans allow you to pay compensation amounts (and their earnings) that will escape Social Security taxation altogether and only be subject to the Medicare tax.
